WANDER:
A horizon shouldn't bend, and here it does, smiling at the lens — which makes the dashcam's default opinion about composition oddly load-bearing. The fisheye dramatizes by reflex; any scene becomes panoramic, faintly sublime, the foreground a stage rake leaning into the viewer. The eighteenth-century analog is the Claude glass, a small tinted convex mirror Gilpin and the picturesque tourists carried in their pockets, named for Claude Lorrain. You turned your back to the actual view, held the mirror up, and looked at the country *backwards through brown glass*, which compressed tonal range, softened distractions, and snapped the scene into a portable rectangle of appropriate-looking landscape. Wordsworth mocked the practice eventually — turning away from the lake to peer at a pocket-sized lake-substitute — because the device had become the lens through which the country was *permitted* to be picturesque, and at some point the country stopped being the point. The dashcam's barrel projection is the same machine, automated and factory-installed, applied to footage no one purchased for its aesthetics; the user bought a security camera and got an unrequested Caspar David Friedrich rider. Phone HDR is the same machine a third time, a calculated compression that reads as "what a good photo looks like" to anyone whose grammar of looking was assembled on Instagram between roughly 2012 and now. A default look is an opinion nobody signed. APC's serif wordmark is a chosen restraint a designer's hand is on — you can name the studio, ask why; the dashcam's curve is an unsigned restraint imposed by a Korean lens vendor whose aesthetic decisions now structure half the documentary footage on the planet, and nobody knows their name and nobody asks. The interesting move is the migration of authorship from named to ambient — the way Helvetica went from a 1957 Haas decision to the unsigned default of public signage, the way the iOS keyboard's autocorrect quietly determines what an entire generation's casual prose can sound like, the way the supermarket's overhead lighting picks the color temperature inside which all produce will be evaluated. Power flows along the unsigned channels, because a signed decision invites argument and an ambient one doesn't.

BRANCH:
The asymmetry between signed and unsigned aesthetic decisions as a quiet politics — whose taste becomes air, and how you'd even argue with air.
