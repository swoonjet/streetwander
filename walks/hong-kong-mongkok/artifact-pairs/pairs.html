Declared & Constituted

Five pairs. Same job, two enforcement modes.
5pairs

Every object carries a rule. Some objects carry it on the surface, addressed to a reader; some carry it in the shape itself, addressed to nobody in particular. The skill of reading a street is noticing which rules each object has agreed to say out loud, and which it has absorbed into its silhouette.

Declarative Rules on the surface. Rules by recognition. Fail gracefully when ignored — merely embarrassed, not broken.
Constitutive Rules in the shape. Rules by being. Non-compliance is physically awkward or impossible.
declared
Pylonus solitaria
The lone traffic cone

Two kilograms of fluorescent paint and a polite civic threat. A cone does not enforce; it attests. Its success depends on ambient civic literacy.

01vs
constituted
Murus saxeus
The concrete Jersey barrier

Invented 1955 on the New Jersey Turnpike because the symbolic kind stopped being enough. Does not ask your opinion. Settles the question with mass.

A city's ratio of cones to barriers is a reliable index of how much it still trusts its inhabitants. Tokyo is cones. The Las Vegas Strip after 2017 is barriers.

declared
Tabula comitis
The museum placard

A cone for artworks. Assumes the visitor already believes they ought not to touch; the sign only reminds them which thing.

02vs
constituted
Vitrina custos
The glass vitrine

A Jersey barrier for art. Has opinions no visitor can argue with. Curatorial aesthetics drift toward the placard in peacetime and back toward the vitrine when the auditor visits.

Contemporary curatorship has migrated toward the placard on the theory that the vitrine insults the visitor. Security directors are migrating back, on the theory that the visitor has been insulting the paintings.

declared
Lex pallida
The speed-limit sign

A number on a metal disc governing the whole road. Addresses an assumed reader at cruising attention, presuming a shared idea of acceptable.

03vs
constituted
Moles asphalti
The speed bump

A sentence in geology. Governs only its own ten meters, at the unmissable cost of your suspension. No readership assumed.

Most traffic-calming disputes are disputes about where the cost of the rule ought to land — on the reader, or on the suspension.

declared
Codex vestium
The dress code at the door

"Jackets required." A rule that admits to being one. Can be broken with a shrug — only the social cost varies.

04vs
constituted
Lux tabulata
180-lux warm downlight

No placard, no rule posted. The ceiling simply refuses to illuminate anyone who arrived in gym shorts at the same register as the jackets. Atmosphere is enforcement that has refused to sign its work.

Most of what we call "atmosphere" is enforcement without signage. Venue designers know this and lean on it because signs kill the register they're trying to produce.

declared
Fotografia edulis
The menu photograph

Retouchable, printable, lit. A promise that is explicitly a promise. The image is downstream of a story and can be corrected.

05vs
constituted
Simulacrum ciborum
The plastic food sample

Sampuru. Hand-carved from resin by a Gujō sculptor, sitting in a window, refusing to decay. Can't be eaten; can't be lied about; can't be pulled down at the next rebrand.

Japan's sampuru industry is a constitutive answer to a problem the West continues to treat declaratively. The wax tempura does the menu-photo's job with better long-term fidelity and worse copy.
