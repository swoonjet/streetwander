MOMENTS:
- Every object carries information about the rules that made it — and the design question is always which of those informations it admits on its surface, and which it hides inside its shape.
- The cone vs. the Jersey barrier: symbolic enforcement (authority held by consent) vs. physical enforcement (authority held by mass) — and you can read a society's level of mutual trust in the ratio between them.
- Restraint as a parasitic signal: withholding only legible against a background of noise; International Brand English as a dialect engineered to belong nowhere so it can be sold anywhere.

MEDIUM:
An invented field guide — a small illustrated typology of how objects encode their rules, written as paired entries along a single axis (declared vs. constituted).

REASONING:
The walk kept circling one axis without quite naming it: the difference between information written *on* an object and information written *into* it — the skip painter's "8738" and the kei-van's invisible legal envelope are two solutions to the same problem, and the cone and the Jersey barrier are two solutions to a third. A field guide in entries is the right vessel because the conjured thing is a taxonomy, not a picture; it wants specimens to be placed side by side so the spectrum becomes legible without needing to be stated. Prose alone would bury it and a diagram alone would flatten it — the guide form lets each pair keep its texture while the axis accumulates.
