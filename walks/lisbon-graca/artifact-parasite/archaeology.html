NP-0 · COMPANION NOTE · §3

A Parasite Archaeology

Bolt-ons that became the brand.
8cases

The feature added in year five is the feature the thing is known for by year fifty. The original spec is almost never the surface that defines the thing at steady state. Parasites graduate. Below, eight cases where the bolt-on became the portrait.

Notre-Dame + Viollet-le-Duc's spire

Architecture · Paris
1163nave→1859spire added

IconicWhen it burned in 2019 the world grieved a twelfth-century building. It was a nineteenth-century pastiche.

Eiffel Tower + not coming down

Architecture · Paris
1889expo pavilion→1909radio antenna kept it

PortraitMeant to be dismantled after twenty years. A military radio antenna bolted to the top made it too useful to remove. The parasite saved the host and became the city's signature.

Slack + threads

Software · SaaS · 2013–
2013team chat→2017threads

CanonOriginally resisted — would fragment the feed. Shipped reluctantly. By year ten, the verb every Slack replacement has to answer for.

Figma + multiplayer cursors

Software · Design tools · 2012–
2012vector pen in a browser→2016live cursors

CanonThe pen was the pitch; the cursors were the retention. Every subsequent tool has had to answer "can we see each other?" first, and "do you have bezier handles?" second.

Spotify + the algorithm

Software · Music · 2008–
2008curated playlists→2015Discover Weekly

CanonThe editorial team was the founding story. By year ten the brand is a vector of listening behaviors, and Discover Weekly is the onboarding demo.

Twitter + RT, @, #

Software · Social · 2006–
2006status update→2007–09user-invented grammar

CanonThe retweet, the @-mention, the hashtag — all invented by users and written over commodity text, then absorbed into the product. The parasites became the syntax.

Pompidou + guts on the outside

Architecture · Paris · 1977
1971art museum brief→1977services exterior by design

IconicRogers and Piano pre-empted the parasite by making the retrofit the facade. The building cannot be tattooed by its own infrastructure because it already is.

Bavarian village + the half-timber revival

Architecture · Europe
800s–1600stimber framing, structural→1880srevival pastiche, decorative

PortraitThe "ancient" Bavarian streetscape most people picture is a late-19th-century revival. The parasite displaced the host in memory — functionally, it became what the thing had always been.
