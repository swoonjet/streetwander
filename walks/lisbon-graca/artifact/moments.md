MOMENTS:
- Every designed form secretly declares a set of native parameters — the distance it expects to be read from, the cardinality it expects to live at, the gesture it was drawn with, the clock it was made under — and mismatches between those hidden declarations and reality register as wrongness the viewer can feel but not name.
- The bolt-on becomes the brand: the surface that defines a thing at year 200 is almost never the surface that was specified at year 0 — it's the corrugated awning, Viollet-le-Duc's spire, Slack's threads — which means the only honest founding move is to leave structural slack for the parasite you can't yet imagine.
- The honest surfaces in a city are the ones that admit their relationship to time — the patch, the scar, the asset tag, the catenary sag — and most dishonesty in design is a surface pretending to be finished.

MEDIUM:
An invented spec — a fake design-system primitive, written as a dry technical document, called "Native Parameters: A Context Declaration for Designed Forms."

REASONING:
The walk kept producing the same shape of argument across eleven blocks — that forms carry hidden declarations about the physical, temporal, or social context they were drawn for, and that naming those declarations upstream changes what gets built. That's not an image, it's an artifact with slots, so the right container is a fake spec — the kind of thing that would sit on a Notion page alongside tokens and components and pretend to be ordinary. The spec format also lets the "bolt-on becomes the brand" idea land where it belongs, as a clause about structural slack, rather than as a separate essay it would be forced to share the page with.
