Non-actionable

Pan-coupé UT-04.3

↑Private → Public (unilateral)
Transfer
Ground-floor floor plate surrendered at the intersection corner to pedestrian eddy. The private undertaking foregoes approx. 14 m² of rentable interior so that the public may turn a corner without colliding with another member of the public.
Notional value
Approx. 90,000 units per corner per annum, rack-rate basis.
Originating deed
Not identified. The Office is unable to locate any instrument under which the surrender was effected, which suggests it has been surrendered many times.

A line item that only reaches the ledger as absence. Absence of interior; absence of invoice.

Non-actionable

Plinth-as-break-room UT-04.1

↓Public → Private (heritage façade → platform-logistics)
Transfer
A heritage plinth, designed c. 1898 to carry the weight of a pilastered elevation, is in daylight hours used as rest furniture by courier operatives affiliated with platform-logistics contractors.
Originating department
Heritage Conservation Board, who were unaware of the use at the time of sampling.
Notional value
Indeterminate. A Legal Adviser footnote holds that valuation may itself be ultra vires.

The plinth was built to carry stone; it has been repurposed to carry the body of a deliverista on shift. Both loads are legitimate. Only one is accounted for.

Non-actionable

Street-tree canopy UT-04.2

↓Public → Public (asset → commons)
Transfer
A mature Ficus, estab. c. 1970, supplies approx. 2,100 m² of dappled jali-shade across the Corridor in peak season. The equivalent aperture, crafted in ornamental stonework, would run into the tens of millions of units.
Book value
Nil. The asset register carries mature street trees at $1,200 (replacement-sapling cost). The Office regards the figure as off by two to three orders of magnitude and has flagged the disagreement.
Originating department
Parks Division.

An object whose present value is a product of the Municipality's past patience. The tree has outlived the registry that would know how to price it.

Non-actionable

Newsstand face-out UT-04.5

↑Private → Public (attention-commons)
Transfer
The façade of the newsstand, at all hours of operation, presents a mosaic of magazine covers face-out. The general public consumes this daily compilation as a shared frame of reference at zero marginal cost; the retailer absorbs the display as a sunk commercial expense.
Notional value
Not calculable. Pricing the attention would pre-empt pending deliberations in two other departments.
Direct beneficiary
Anyone with a line of sight.

A civic by-product of private retail. The publisher prints the cover to sell the magazine; the city receives the surface for free.

Non-actionable

Valet pedestal UT-04.6

↔Private ↔ Private (mediated by municipal surface)
Transfer
A valet-parking service whose fixed capital stock consists of a printed board, a stand, and an understanding with the adjacent restaurant. No licence is on file for the use of the public way.
Notional value
Approx. 11,000 units per annum (estimated).
Legal basis
The Office was unable to identify one.

An enterprise operating entirely in the gap between two deeds. Its business is the gap.

Non-actionable

Bolero station UT-04.7

↔Private ↔ Private (mediated by municipal surface)
Transfer
A sidewalk shoeshine position, long-standing, commensal on the court-adjacent block. Analogous to UT-04.6 in structure, though with a different capital stock (brass footrests, wooden toolbox, generations of accumulated clientele).
Notional value
Approx. 4,500 units per annum (estimated).
Precedent
Predates the Corridor's pedestrianisation by some decades. The pedestrianisation inherited it.

An occupant who predates the arrangement under which they occupy. The most durable class of unbilled transfer.
