MOMENTS:
- Cities run on unsigned substrates — governance by fatigue, by witness, by transaction-spillover — and the legible civic layer (statutes, streetlamps, ordinances) is a thin crust over something older and bottom-up
- The missing middle duration: between stone-time (centuries) and paper-time (days) there is textile-time, the six-month banner, the layer where civic mood actually registers and that regimes panic about
- The implicit has a permission the explicit can't reproduce — the kerb seats people a bench cannot, and the moment an affordance gets a label it costs half its usefulness

MEDIUM:
Invented field guide — a glossary of unsigned civic protocols

REASONING:
The walk kept arriving, from different angles, at the same suspicion: that the parts of a city which actually function run on protocols nobody has written down (arm-height waterlines, sittable edges, the edicola-votiva maintenance loop, the lamp at the bend of a tunnel), and that formalizing them tends to kill them. A glossary is the honest vessel because it treats these patterns as a real inventory worth naming — which is what the walk itself was trying to do. Prose would dilute them into anecdote; a diagram would flatten them into nodes. Let them be entries, each a name for something the dictionary was missing.
