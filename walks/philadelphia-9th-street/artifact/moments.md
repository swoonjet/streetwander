MOMENTS:
- The compression gradient from arcade → canopy → awning → string-light catenary: function subtracted, meaning preserved, architecture reduced to punctuation.
- A family of lightweight signals doing load-bearing work — the lone cone, the chalk-marker NDUJA, the April Christmas tree, the unlit bulbs — each one heavier in semantics than in matter.
- Referent-drift as a slow phase change: any object kept past its calendar accrues a second meaning, and if kept long enough the second one becomes primary and the first becomes folklore.

MEDIUM:
An invented field guide — a short typological catalog of "compressed vessels," each entry naming a species of lightweight object doing structural semantic work, with notes on its older heavier ancestor and its compression ratio.

REASONING:
The walk kept compressing things — a roof into a dotted line of bulbs, a crossing guard into a flashing LED, a saint into a tree — and noticing that meaning survives the loss of mass with surprising fidelity. A field guide is the right vessel because the thought itself is taxonomic: a family resemblance across objects that don't otherwise belong together (a cone, a chalkboard, an April relic, a string light), held together by the rule of progressive subtraction. Writing it as a guide rather than an essay lets the form mirror the content — each entry is itself a compression, a heavyweight observation rendered as a lightweight specimen card.
