The Prop Register

Six members of the same quiet category.
6members

The category R. named in his letter: the prop that accompanies a signal into a medium the signal cannot travel through unassisted. The lunar flag was the first instance filed on live television. Below are five others that kept the door open afterward — and one that walked through it well before any camera was invented.

I · 1969

The lunar flag

Apollo 11 · Sea of Tranquility · televised
Signal
this is a flag, and it has been planted
Missing medium
atmosphere
Prop
seven-eighths-inch aluminum rod, telescoping, sewn into the upper hem. 0.028-inch wall.
Disclosure
none — no plaque on the Sea of Tranquility saying contains a stiffening member, without which it would hang straight down.
Confessed
the ripple at the fly. The rod did not fully extend; the cloth folded once. "My one small contribution to honesty," R. writes.
II · 1980s–

The night-lit cornice

post-war downtowns · every major city · dusk
Signal
architectural ornament, legible from the street
Missing medium
daylight
Prop
uplighters hidden in the sidewalk, washing the cornice from below in a register the 1890s architect never drew.
Disclosure
credits in the spec sheet; invisible to the pedestrian.
Who it is for
the nocturnal passerby the original drawing did not anticipate. R.'s retired schoolteacher, out for an evening.
III · 1990s–

The showroom dashboard

car dealership · after hours · empty cabin
Signal
the car is alive; it knows where it is
Missing medium
a driver, a key in the ignition, a satellite fix
Prop
demo mode — an animated GPS route played on loop. The car is stationary; the map moves anyway.
Newer variant
the route is now chosen by a model predicting which animation the viewer will find most compelling.
Lineage
direct descendant. Same rod, larger hem.
IV · 1930s–

The foley footstep

post-production · Hollywood · soundstage
Signal
footsteps — a body in space
Missing medium
the actual location's audio, which was captured for dialogue only
Prop
a foley artist, props, a sandbox of gravel, a pit of concrete, matched in post to onscreen action.
Disclosure
a credit deep in the roll; audible as an industry joke, not a deception.
Oldest variant
Shakespeare's cloth-in-wind and stage-thunder sheets. The translation predates the camera.
V · 2000s–

The demo-mode panel

big-box retailer · calibrated best case
Signal
this is what the television looks like
Missing medium
the viewer's future content (cable broadcast, streaming compression, living-room light)
Prop
saturated nature footage and a dynamic-range pass the panel will never see again after it is taken home.
Afterlife
every subsequent panel the viewer encounters is measured against the showroom's rod. The rod becomes the honest reference.
VI · c. 1790

The painted cannon port

Atlantic merchant shipping · pre-telegraph
Signal
this ship is armed; do not attempt to board
Missing medium
an affordable cannon, and the crew to work it
Prop
black-painted squares with circular barrels, spaced along the hull to mimic a gun deck.
Audience
privateers at distance; the paint worked out to several miles of effective deterrence.
Relevance
the category is at least as old as paint on wood. R. didn't invent the rod. He was the first to install one on live television.
